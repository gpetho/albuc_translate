Chapter forty-six on cauterizing the uvula.
If pain occurs in some organs due to a fall or a blow, and that pain lasts for a long time, and treatment with medications is not effective
then it should be cauterized with a point cautery, and the cauterization of each organ should be according to its size, smallness, weakness, strength, and the severity and chronicity of the pain.
If it heals from the first cauterization, then fine, otherwise, repeat the cauterization because it is the nature of these pains to move from the organ to what is near it.
Therefore, it is necessary to follow it with cauterization until the patient recovers.
