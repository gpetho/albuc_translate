of back pain
When pain comes to the back, either by falling or by a blow, or by too great an evacuation, or by some spread of cold matter, for none of these cases is cautery useful, except for the one that comes from the infusion of cold matter.
It is necessary after the patient has been purged with foul-smelling pills or other such, that three lines marked with ink be made on his back where the pain is, and on each line five cauteries or more according to the strength and amount that the patient can bear; and let the cautery be done according to the cautery which is called "points"; and if you wish, make three or four cauteries with a medium key cautery according to this form:
