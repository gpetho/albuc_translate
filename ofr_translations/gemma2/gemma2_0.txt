O vos, tres chiers filz, je vos ai acomplit cest livre qui est li derriens de la science de medicine ensamble l’emplissement de lui, et ai fait la fin per ses expositions et par ses declarations.	O you, three dear sons, I have completed this book which is the last of the science of medicine in its entirety, and have made an end through its expositions and through its declarations.  
Et m’est venu en vision que je le vos acomplisse avec cest traitier, qui est partie de l’uevre de mains, c’est a savoir «cyrurgie».	And it came to me in vision that I complete it for you with this treatise, which is part of the work of hands, that is to say "surgery". 
Et por ceu que l’uevre de mains est fortraite en nostre region et est etraingie dou tout en tout, si est perie li science et decopee, et n’est riens demorez de li fuers que les petites descriptions en livres des anciens que les mains ont muees.	And because the work of hands is fortified in our region and is strange to all around, so it has perished the science and been cut off, and nothing remains of its force except for the small descriptions in books of the ancients that have been moved by hands.  
Et de ceu est venue li error et li vanitez, et sont closes les ententions et est aloignie li science.	And from this came the error and the vanity, and intentions are closed and the science is alienated. 
Or me semble que je la vuel vivifier ensamble l’ordination de cest traitier selonc la voie d’exposition et de declaration et de l’abreviation;	Or it seems to me that I bring it to life together with the ordering of this treatise according to the way of exposition and declaration and abbreviation; 
et venrai ensamble les formes des ferremens de cautere, et des autres estrumens de l’uevre avec l’ajostement de la declaration et de preparation de li, sicom il est mestier.	and I will bring together the forms of the instruments of cautery, and of the other instruments of the work with the adjustment of the declaration and preparation of them, as is necessary. 
Et por la raison de ceu que nuls boins maistres de mains n’est trovez en nostre tens, est li ars de medicine eslongié.	And for the reason of those that no good masters of hands are found in our time, the art of medicine is lengthened.
Et covient celui qui s’en entremet sormonter en la science d’anathomie, si com Galiens dit, tant qu’il saiche la juement des menbres, et les formes et les conjunctions et les separations d’aus, et aient connissance des osses et des nerf et des brais et le nombre d’ous, et le demenement et des voines movans et des reposans, et dou leu de lor issue.	And it is necessary for him who undertakes this to climb in the science of anatomy, as Galen says, until he knows the judgment of the members, and the forms and the conjunctions and separations of veins, and have knowledge of the bones and nerves and brains and the number of vessels, and the movement and of moving and resting veins, and their place of origin. 
Et por ceu dist Ypocras que li nons avec le non est mout, et avec l’uevre est poc;	And for this, Hippocrates says that the name with the name is bad, and with the work it is little. 
et meismement en l’art de main.	And likewise in the art of hand. 
Nos vos disons en l’introite de cest livre que qui n’est conissans des choses devant dites, si se gart qu’il ne chiece en error et qu’il n’ocie les homes;	We say to you in the introduction of this book that whoever is not familiar with the things said before, he should guard himself so that he does not err and harm people.
ensi que j’ai veut maintes fois plusors de ces qui s’entremettent de ceste science et si s’abandonent sen science et sens experiment.	Thus as I have often seen many who meddle in this science and abandon their own knowledge and experience.   
Car je vis un fol mire qui copa I apostume de scrophules ou col d’une femme, et copa les arteres dou col;	For I saw a fool who cut an abscess of scrofula or neck of a woman, and cut the arteries of the neck.  
por coi tant de sanc issit hors que li femme cheit morte entre ses mains.	Therefore so much blood came out that the woman died in his hands.  
Et vis I autre mire qui traït une piere d’un home d’eaige, et li piere estoit grans;	And I saw another surgeon who treated a stone of a man with water, and the stone was large;
si emporta avec li une piece de la vessie, et fut mors li hons dedens III jors.	If he carries with him a piece of bladder, and was killed the man inside three days.  
Et je i avoie ja esteit apelez por lou traire, et vis la disposition dou malaide et la grandor de la piere, et soi bien s’on lou tailloit il en morroit.	And I had already been called to treat him, and saw the disposition of the patient and the size of the stone, and well if one cut it he would die. 
Et vis I autre mire qui prist en cure I duc de nostre terre qui avoit la jambe brisie, et avec la briseure ot plaie;	And seeing another mire who took care of the duke of our land who had a broken leg, and with the fracture there was a wound; 
et li mires plains d’ignorance estraint la brisure sus la plaie as esteles par fort lieure, et ne lassa a la plaie point de xalain, et le lassa ensi I termine;	And the mire full of ignorance forced the fracture over the wound to the bones by very strong ligature, and did not leave on the wound any lint, and left him thus at the end;
et li comanda qu’il ne la desliast mie;	and he commanded that he should not untie it. 
et demora ensi que li jambe fut toute enflee et li piez et vint a perdition.	And remained thus that the leg was completely swollen and the foot, and came to ruin.  
Je fuis apelez por venir a lui, et me hastai por venir deslier la jambe;	I was called to come to him, and hastened to come to untie the leg. 
et quant je l’oi desloie si fut em pais et ne sentit point de dolor.	and when I moved it, it was at peace and felt no pain. 
Mais toute voie li corruptions estoit ja confermee ou membre, et ne la poi saner;	But all signs of corruption were already confirmed in the member, and I could not heal it; 
et ne cessa li corrution d’aler par le membre tant que li membres fut dou tout perdus.	and the corruption did not cease to spread through the limb until the limb was entirely lost.  
Et vis I autre mire qui effondra I apostume enchancreit, et puis enflama per le malice de sa signorie.	And behold another case where the abscess burst open and then inflamed due to the evil of its lord. 
Car chancre, quant il est purs d’umor melancolique, ne covient mie qu’on i(l) touche de fer s’il n’est en teil leu c’on puisse toute oster ce que est corrumpus.	For a sore, when it is pure of melancholic humor, it is not good to touch it with iron unless in such a place where one can entirely remove what is corrupted.  
Et por ceu, treschier filz, vos est besoig que vos sachiez que oevre de mains est devisee en II divisions:	And for this reason, dear son, it is necessary that you know that the work of hands is divided into two parts:
c’est asavoir, huevre ou il a peril en plusors dispositions;	that is to say, work which has danger in many positions; 
et je mostrerai en chescun leu de cest livre ou il a paour et peril.	And I will show in each place of this book where there is fear and peril. 
Por coi il est besoig que vos vos en gardez, si que li fol mire ne puissent mal parler de vos.	Therefore it is necessary that you guard against it, so that fools may not speak evil of you.  
Si prenez vos armes en cusenson, et entreprenez vos malaides legierement et fermement, et usez de la millor voie si que per la graice de Deu vos venez a salut et soiez loez;	So take up your arms with compassion, and undertake your patients lightly and firmly, and use the best way so that by the grace of God you come to salvation and be praised. 
et lassiez les maladies espoantables de grief saniteit;	And leave the diseases curable to proper health;
