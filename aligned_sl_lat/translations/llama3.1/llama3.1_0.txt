Chapter 1.
But before mentioning the operation with him, it is necessary that I say what are the qualities of his cauterizations and their harm; and in which complexion they are shown.
I say therefore that the speech about the quality of cauterization and its harm is a long and subtle discourse, requiring knowledge and a hidden secret.
And many wise men have spoken about it, and they disagreed on the matter; and for my part I am satisfied with little from their words, fearing prolongation.
I say therefore that cauterization is applied universally to all humors, whether with matter or without matter; except for two complexions, which are hot humor without matter and dry humor without matter.
They disagreed, however, in the case of warm and dry complexion with matter.
For some said that cauterization is beneficial in it.
And others said the opposite of this, namely that cauterization is not suitable in a disease caused by warmth and dryness; because the nature of fire is to be warm: and it is improper for healing to occur from a hot and dry affliction with heat and dryness.
And he who says the opposite of this said that by cauterization a benefit is achieved in every hot and dry disease which occurs in human bodies: because when you compare the body of man and his humors to the nature of fire, you find the body of man to be cold.
And I myself say this by his words because experience has already revealed it to me a number of times.
However, it is not necessary for one to come upon that [matter], unless he has already been proven and exercised in the last exercise of the chapter on Cauterization, and knows the diversity of human complexions, and the arrangement of afflictions within themselves, and their causes, and accidents, and the length of time of them.

And know, sons, that from the secret of cure by Cautery with fire and its goodness over Cautery with burning medicine there is a difference; because fire is simple, whose action does not reach except to the member which is cauterized: and it does not harm another member joined to it, unless by slight injury.
