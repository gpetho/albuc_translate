When diarrhea comes from cold and moisture, so much so that the retentive and digestive power which is in the stomach and in the intestine is weakened, and cannot be treated by any means of treatment; and you see the patient suffering greatly and of strong constitution, make a large cautery on the stomach according to what has been discussed previously on cautery of the stomach, with the circular cautery; and four cauteries around the navel, small, with a small key-shaped cautery, and two cauteries on the hips.
And if you see abundant moisture and the patient suffering, make a cautery on the wrist, and a cautery on each flank, and two small cauteries on the stomach near the large one, because the treatment is beneficial when there is good assistance.
