of the dislocation of the knee
The knee is dislocated in three ways: that is to say, inward and outward, and backward;
and does not dislocate forward.
And the sign is that you instruct the patient to bend their leg at the hip, and if it does not fit at the hip, know that it is dislocated;
and the restoration of all is that you make the patient sit upright, and extend their leg if they can, and place your back against their face, and put their foot on your feet;
then take their knee in your palms, and pull between your fingers, then bend the two sides of the knee firmly; and another assistant extends their foot, so that the knee returns to its place, and the sign is that their leg fits well at their hip easily;
and place the plaster on it, and make the leg fit at the hip, and bind them together tightly for three or four days, then unbind it;
and the patient does not attempt to walk, until the limb is strengthened.
And if the limb is not completely restored, the assistant should apply strong stretching, as we have said in the treatment of the hip, until it is healed.
of the dislocation of the heel.
The heel is sometimes displaced, and sometimes dislocated completely, both inwards and outwards.
And the signs are that the ankle is swollen, and protruding towards the part where it is dislocated.
The reduction is gentle, and one should gently stretch it with one's hands when it is only displaced.
And when it is completely dislocated, you must have the patient sit upright, and two assistants hold firmly the leg, above the ankle.
then pull the foot towards your right hand, and then to your left hand twice, then extend it upwards, and gently push the foot upwards.

And if boils appear there, you must relieve them, as we have said elsewhere, until they are healed.

of wounds and dislocations together
When a wound comes with the dislocation, and one wishes to restore it, often the patient dies.
Do not be presumptuous in the treatment, if you are not skilled in the art and have not practiced it for a long time, and if you are not compassionate and merciful and slow, and not rash.
and use soothing things at the beginning for hot abscesses, and leave the patient in God's hands if you see no hope of health. And when you see hope, then restore the limb to its place, before the hot abscess comes there.
And when the limb is in place, administer soothing things to the hot abscess, and treat the wound, as is proper, with drying ointments.
And if with the dislocation there is a fracture, and small bones separated, then set them, and do what we have said in simple ailments.
And guard yourself from entering a path of fear, according to what I have said, for this will be to your detriment according to your Creator, and for the salvation of your soul and body.
