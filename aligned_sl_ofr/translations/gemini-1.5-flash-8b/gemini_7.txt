of the dislocation of the knee
The knee is dislocated in three ways: that is to say, inward and outward, and backward;
and it does not dislocate forward.
And the sign is that you instruct the patient to bend their leg at the hip, and if it does not fit against their hip, know that it is dislocated;
And the restoration of all is that you make the patient sit upright, and extend their leg if they can, and place your back against their thigh, and put their foot on your feet;
then take their knee in your palms, and pull between your fingers, then bend the two sides of the knee tightly; and another assistant extends their foot, so that the knee returns to its place, and the sign is that they fit their leg against their hip lightly;
and place the plaster on it, and make the leg fit against the hip, and bind them together firmly for three or four days, then unbind them;
and the patient does not work walking, until the limb is strengthened.
And if the limb is not quite right, the assistant applies strong stretching, as we have said in the treatment of the hip, until it is healed.
of the dislocation of the heel
The heel is sometimes displaced, and sometimes dislocated completely, both inside and outward.
And the signs are that the ankle is swollen, and it bulges forward to the part where it is dislocated:
The repositioning is gentle, and one should gently stretch it with one's hands when it is merely displaced.
And when it is completely dislocated, you must make the patient sit upright, and have two assistants hold the leg firmly above the ankle;
then pull the foot towards your right hand, and then to the left twice, then stretch it upwards, and place the foot firmly upwards;
and do this twice, as we have said, pushing and pulling the foot;
if it returns, that is good;
if not, then start the work again, for it returns;
and if it cannot return thus, then make the sick person lie on their back on the ground, and firmly place a stick in the ground between their thighs, and tie cloths around it so that it does not injure the sick person;
then let the attendants hold the thigh, and another attendant extend the foot to their hands or to a rope, that they tie around the top of the foot; then let each attendant pull equally, and the stick be always between the sick person's thighs to hold the sick person when they are being stretched;
then let the surgeon place the dislocation in their hands, and another attendant hold the healthy leg underneath, and the dislocation will return quickly.
And when it is in place, then put the plaster and bandages on, and bind it tightly, and tie the foot to the leg, and guard the nerves that are above the ankle behind, lest the bandage be too tight;
then leave it for two or three days, and if the bandage softens, then tighten it;
then untie it on the third or fourth day, and forbid the patient from walking for four days; and if he walks before that time, it is not certain that it will not dislocate again.
And if boils appear there, you must soothe them, as we have said elsewhere, until they are healed.
of the fingers When the fingers are dislocated, they must be gently straightened without effort, and it is an easy matter.
And if the dislocation is in any bone of the back of the foot, you must have the patient sit on a flat surface or a table, completely straight, and place your foot on the exposed part of the joints, then press firmly with your foot until it returns;
and when it is aligned and nothing is apparent, place a table with two supports under the sole of the foot, then hold it firmly, and leave it for three days, then release it; and keep it at rest until it is healed.
of wound and dislocation together
When a wound comes with the dislocation, and one wants to restore it, many times the patient dies:
So do not be presumptuous in the treatment, if you are not wise in the art and have not used it for a long time, and if you are not compassionate and merciful and slow, and not hasty;
and use at the beginning soothing things for hot abscesses, and leave the patient in God's hand if you see no hope of health; And when you see hope, then restore the limb to its place, before the hot abscesses come to it;
And when the limb is in place, administer soothing things for hot abscesses, and treat the wound, as it is fitting, with drying ointments;
And if with the dislocation there is a fracture, and small bones separated, then set them, and do what we have said in simple illnesses;
And guard yourself from entering a path of fear, according to what I have said, for this will earn you praise from your Creator, and for the salvation of your body and soul.
