Chapter twenty-four on cauterizing lung disease and cough.
If the cough and lung disease are due to cold humors, and the patient has neither fever nor phthisis, and the disease is chronic, then cauterize him with two cauterizations above the clavicles in the low, soft, empty places, and another cauterization in the middle of the chest between the breasts, and the cautery iron should be nail-shaped in the form that has been previously described.
And if you wish, let your cauterization be by spotting with the cautery iron that is called "the point," and its form has been previously described in the spotting for earache, and the spots should be from thirty spots to about that number.
Then treat him with what has been mentioned previously until he recovers.
And a cautery iron with three prongs may be made in this form, and cauterization is hastened with it because you cauterize three cauterizations with it at once.
