Chapter thirty-five on the extraction of the frog generated under the tongue.
A swelling may occur under the tongue, resembling a small frog, which prevents the tongue from its natural function.
And perhaps it grows until it fills the mouth,
and the treatment for it is to open the patient's mouth facing the sun and examine the swelling. If you see it dark in color or black and hard, and the patient feels no sensation in it, do not touch it, for it is cancer.
But if it is tending towards white and has moisture in it, insert a hook into it and incise it with a fine scalpel and free it from every side,
If the bleeding overwhelms you during your work, put powdered vitriol on it until the bleeding stops, then return to your work until you remove it completely.
Then have him rinse his mouth with vinegar and salt, then treat him with all the other appropriate remedies until he recovers.
