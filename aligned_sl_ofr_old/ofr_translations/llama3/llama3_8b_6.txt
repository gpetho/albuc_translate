and do this twice, as we have said, by pushing and pulling the foot; if it returns, that's good; if not, then start again, for it will return.
and if it cannot return thus, make the patient sit on their back on the ground, and put one pillow firmly in the ground between their thighs, and tie bands around, so that they do not harm the patient.
then have one assistant hold the thigh, and another assistant stretch the foot to their hands or to a strap, which they tie around the ankle of the foot. then stretch each assistant evenly, and the pillows be placed between the patient's thighs to retain the patient when one is stretching.
then place the mirrors evenly on the dislocation in their hands, and one other assistant holds the healthy leg below, and the dislocation will return quickly.
And when it is at point, then apply the poultice and bandage on top, and tie strongly, and bind the foot to the leg, and watch the nerves which are behind the ankle, so that the ligament is not too tight.
then leave it for two or three days, and if the ligament has loosened, then strain it; then release it on the third or fourth day, and forbid the patient to walk for three days. and if they go before that term, it is not surety that it will not dislocate again.
And if abscesses come up, then you must calm them, as we have said elsewhere, until they are healed.
of the elbows
When the elbows are dislocated, it is well to stretch them gently without effort, and that is a light thing.
And if the dislocation is in any part of the back of the foot, then it is necessary that you make the patient sit on one level or on a table completely straight, and place your foot on what appears to be these joints, then press with your foot strongly, until it returns.
and when it is straight and there is nothing apparent, then place under the sole of the foot a board on two chairs, then strain strongly, and leave it for three days, then release it; and let it rest until it is healed.
of wound and dislocation together
When a wound comes with the dislocation, and one wants to restore it, many times it happens that the patient dies:
if you are not presumptuous in the treatment, if you are not wise in the art and have not used it for a long time, and if you are not pitiful and merciful and slow, nor hasty.
and at the beginning, use soothing things on the abscess, and leave the patient in God's hand, if you do not see hope of recovery. and when you see hope, then reset the member to its place, as long as the abscess is coming.
and when the member is at its place, then administer soothing things to the abscess, and treat the wound with dried ointments, as is fitting.
and if there is breaking with the dislocation, and small bones dislocated, then set them, and do what we have said in simple diseases.
and beware of falling into a path of fear, as I have said, for this is worthily done according to your Creator, and for the wisdom of your arm and body. Amen.
