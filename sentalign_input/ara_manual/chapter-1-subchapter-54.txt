Chapter Fifty-Four.
On Cauterizing a Tremor.
If someone experiences tremors from a chill in a nerve or from quartan fever or other causes, it is necessary to cauterize him four or five times on the spinal vertebrae, one cauterization between each vertebra, and one cauterization on his chest.
And one cauterization on his stomach with the olive-shaped cautery. The tremors will cease, and this speeds up the resolution of the cold ailment.
