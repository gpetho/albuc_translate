of the veins of the eyes
Specks are red veins that cover the eye, and impede vision and constantly weaken the eye.
It is therefore necessary first that you see if the eye, in which the speck is, is strong, and there is no other disease besides the speck; then lift it in this way: instruct the patient to put his head in your lap, then lift these veins with the hook according to your skill, and let the hook be a fine double hook according to this form:
And let there be two hooks of different sizes on one handle in this form:
then lift these veins carefully with the hooks, and release the blood little by little, until you see these veins recede from the eye; and be careful not to injure the eye with the points of the hooks, and do this work at midday in the sun, and be very careful in your work so that you only cut these veins; and when you have done this, instill green and red collyrium into the eye to destroy what remains of the speck.
And if you cannot remove it all at that time, apply a plaster on the eye of things that soothe the pain of the eyes, and leave it for a few days until the pain subsides and be sure of a warm abscess, then start again in the same way as before until it is healed; and this is the form of the hooks:
