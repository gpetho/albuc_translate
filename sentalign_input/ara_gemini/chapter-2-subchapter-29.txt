Chapter twenty-nine on filing the teeth with iron.
Rough, ugly scales may gather on the surfaces of the teeth, inside and out, and between the gums.
And they may blacken, yellow, and turn green until corruption reaches the gums and the teeth become ugly because of that.
So, you should sit the patient between your hands with his head in your lap and scrape the molar and the tooth in which the scales and the thing resembling sand have appeared to you until nothing of it remains.
And likewise, you do the same with the blackness, the greenness, the yellowness, and other things until you clean it.
If what is in it is gone from the first scraping, then fine, otherwise you repeat the scraping on it another day, and a second, and a third, until you reach the goal in what you want.
And know that the molar needs scrapers of various forms and many shapes according to what is prepared for your work because the scraper with which the molar is scraped from the inside is different from the scraper with which it is scraped from the outside.
And the one with which it is scraped between the molars is of another form.
And these are several forms of scrapers that you should have, all prepared.
