Of the treatment by cupping, and the quality of its use.
Chapter 48.
Cupping glasses are made from horns, and from copper, and from glass.
And cupping is done in two ways indeed.
With scarification and bloodletting; and the other is cupping without scarification. And this cupping which is without scarification, is in two modes.
Or it is with fire.
Or it is without fire.
And the cuppings which are administered with scarification and bloodletting are fourteen places of the body.
Of which one is the cupping of Alnocrati, and it is at the back of the head; and Alkehel, and it is in the middle of the neck; and the cuppings of Alhagdahim, and they are two layers of the neck from both sides together; and the cupping of the mind, and it is under the jawbone in the lower jaw; and the cupping of the two spatulas; and the cupping of Alachosos above the end of the tailbone; and the cupping of the two fossae, and they are in the middle of the two arms; and the cupping of the two thighs; and the cupping of the two hips.
And indeed the cupping does not extract blood from you through the small scattered veins in the flesh, and because of that, no virtue falls out from them, just as it is the case with phlebotomy.
It is not fitting to administer a treatment with fire or without fire for any ailment that arises from excess, until the whole body is evacuated.
Because if necessity provokes ventilation due to ailment or because of habit, we administer it at every hour, at the beginning of the month, at its end, and in the middle, and whenever the time may be.
Because it is so, since there are people who, when the blood increases in them, need its extraction through ventilation, and find heaviness in their head, and swoon.
And there are those who find fullness and redness in their face, head, and neck.
And there are those who find itchiness on their face and forehead, and darkness, and dullness in their eyes.
And there are those who scratch the place of their ventilation. And there are those whose laughter increases.
And there are those who find the taste of blood in their mouth, or their tongue becomes putrid and spits out blood.
And there are those whose sleep increases.
And there are those who see in their sleep blood and redness, and wounds, and sores, and things similar to these. When therefore we see something of these, and it is specifically in the middle third of the month, we prescribe then that ventilation should be done, after two or three hours have passed. The instrument of ventilation of the neck is this: because it contributes to heaviness in the head and what is poured out to the eyes.
