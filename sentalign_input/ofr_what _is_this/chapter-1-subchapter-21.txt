of the pain of the teeth
When toothache comes from cold, where there is cold or a worm, and medicinal treatments are ineffective, then cautery is necessary in two ways: cautery with butter and cautery with fire.
Cautery with butter is such that you take cow's butter, and melt it in an iron or brass spoon; then take cotton and wrap it around the tip of a tent, and soak it in boiling butter, and apply it quickly to the aching tooth and hold it there until it cools; and do this often until its virtue reaches the root of the tooth, and if you wish also soak wool and cotton in cold butter, and place it on the aching tooth, and apply a hot iron until the heat reaches the end of the tooth.
The cautery with fire is as follows: you take a rod of iron or brass, thick enough so that the fire does not reach the patient's mouth; then heat the cautery, the shape of which will come later, and place it on the tooth and hold your hand there until the cautery cools; do this often, for the pain will be removed that same day or the next.
