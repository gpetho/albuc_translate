of the pain of the teeth
When toothache comes from cold, or there is cold or a worm, and medical treatments are ineffective, then cautery is necessary in two ways:
and cautery with butter and cautery with fire.
Cautery with butter is as follows: you take cow's butter and melt it in an iron or bronze spoon;
then take cotton and wrap it around the tip of a tent, and soak it in boiling butter, and quickly place it on the aching tooth and hold it there until it cools;
and do this often until its virtue reaches the root of the tooth, and if you wish, soak wool and cotton in cold butter, and place it on the aching tooth, and place a hot iron on it until the heat reaches the end of the tooth.
The cautery with combined fire is as follows: you take a tube of iron or bronze, and it should be thick so that the fire does not reach the patient's mouth;
then heat the cautery, the shape of which will come later, and place it on the tooth and hold your hand there until the cautery cools;
do this often, for the pain will be removed that same day or the next.
